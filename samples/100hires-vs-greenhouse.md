# 100Hires vs Greenhouse: Which ATS Is Better for Startups in 2026?

*Choosing an applicant tracking system is one of the first scaling decisions a startup makes. Pick the wrong one and you're either overpaying for features you won't use or outgrowing a tool that can't keep up. Here's how 100Hires and Greenhouse compare — with an honest look at where each one wins.*

---

## The Short Version

**100Hires** is built for small and mid-sized teams that want a capable ATS without enterprise pricing or complexity. **Greenhouse** is the industry standard for structured hiring at scale — but that structure comes at a cost, literally and operationally.

If you're a startup hiring your first 50 people, 100Hires gets you moving fast. If you're a 200-person company building a recruiting org with DE&I mandates and compliance requirements, Greenhouse is hard to beat.

## Where 100Hires Wins

### 1. Pricing That Makes Sense for Startups

The gap here is massive. A 30-person startup pays $49–$199/month for 100Hires. The same company would spend $6,000–$8,000/year on Greenhouse's Essential plan — plus implementation fees that can add another $3,000–$10,000 in year one.

100Hires uses flat-rate pricing with no per-user fees. Every recruiter, hiring manager, and interviewer gets access without budget anxiety. For a startup watching burn rate, this matters.

### 2. Speed to Value

100Hires works out of the box. Sign up, post a job, start tracking candidates — same day. Greenhouse requires a structured implementation process that typically takes 5–6 weeks, including training sessions, workflow configuration, and data migration.

For a startup that needs to hire three engineers *this month*, five weeks of setup isn't realistic.

### 3. Chrome Extension for Sourcing

100Hires' Chrome extension lets recruiters import candidates directly from LinkedIn, GitHub, and Meetup. It recognizes existing candidates in your pipeline and shows notes and status — so you never accidentally reach out to someone who's already in process.

The extension also includes a contact finder waterfall that runs candidates through five tools to discover personal emails and phone numbers, claiming 37% more contacts found than single-tool approaches.

### 4. Built-in Outreach Tools

Email sequences, SMS, voicemail drops, and automated follow-ups are included in the Advanced plan ($199/mo). Greenhouse requires third-party integrations or higher-tier plans for comparable outreach capabilities.

---

## Where Greenhouse Wins

### 1. Structured Hiring at Scale

Greenhouse doesn't just track applicants — it enforces a hiring methodology. Every role gets a hiring plan with defined competencies, interview stages, and evaluation criteria before a single resume comes in. This reduces bias, improves consistency, and generates data that actually means something.

For companies hiring 50+ roles per year across multiple teams, this structure prevents the chaos that unstructured hiring creates.

### 2. DE&I Tools

Greenhouse leads the industry here. Anonymous resume reviews, demographic tracking, bias-reduction nudges during evaluations, and compliance reporting (NYC Local Law 144, EU AI Act) are built into the platform. If your startup has DE&I commitments or regulatory requirements, this is a genuine differentiator.

### 3. Integration Ecosystem

With 500+ native integrations, Greenhouse connects to virtually every tool in a modern recruiting stack: HRIS platforms (Workday, BambooHR, ADP), video tools (Zoom, Teams), assessment platforms, background check providers, and collaboration tools (Slack). 100Hires covers the basics with Zapier, but can't match this breadth natively.

### 4. Enterprise-Grade AI

Greenhouse offers 16+ AI features including offer acceptance forecasting, talent matching against calibrated criteria, and resume anonymization for bias reduction. These are meaningful tools for high-volume recruiting teams. 100Hires' AI features are solid for the price but narrower in scope.

---

## The Honest Tradeoffs

**Greenhouse's weaknesses** are well-documented: reporting scored just 3.2/5 on Capterra despite being a data-driven platform, mobile apps are underpowered, and 43% of negative reviews cite high cost for smaller businesses. The careers page builder is basic enough that many companies build custom alternatives.

**100Hires' weaknesses** are what you'd expect at the price point: reporting is limited, resume parsing can be inconsistent, and the platform lacks the deep compliance and DE&I tooling that regulated industries need. It's also less proven at scale — most success stories come from teams under 200 people.

---

## Verdict

**Choose 100Hires if** you're a startup or SMB hiring up to 200 people, you want to move fast without a lengthy implementation, and you'd rather spend $200/month than $10,000/year. The AI features and sourcing tools punch well above their price point.

**Choose Greenhouse if** you're scaling past 200 employees, you need structured hiring with compliance and DE&I built in, and you have the budget and timeline for a full implementation. The methodology is the product — the ATS is just the delivery mechanism.

For most startups reading this in 2026: start with 100Hires, switch to Greenhouse when the pain of unstructured hiring exceeds the cost of structured implementation.
