# LinkedIn Algorithm Key Findings (2024–2026)

Based on Richard van der Blom's Algorithm Insights Reports — the only large-scale, data-driven analyses of LinkedIn's algorithm. Data cited below comes from the 2024 report (1.5M+ posts analyzed) and the 2026 report (1.3M posts, Sept 2025–March 2026).

- **Source**: Algorithm Insights Report, published annually by Just Connecting
- **Why this matters**: Every other expert's tactical advice should be validated against this data. Opinions are abundant; large-sample data is not.

---

## The Interest Graph Shift

The single most important structural change on LinkedIn in the past two years.

| Year | Distribution to connections | Distribution via Interest Graph |
|------|---------------------------|-------------------------------|
| 2024 | 70% | 5% |
| 2025 | 54% | 21% |
| 2026 | 10% | 50% |

**What this means for B2B SaaS**: Your post no longer travels through your network first — it travels through your topic cluster. The algorithm asks "what does this person stand for?" not "who does this person know?" Niche clarity and content consistency now outweigh network size.

---

## The Follower Paradox

Follower counts grew 31% across the platform, but impressions fell.

This is not a bug — it is a deliberate architectural decision by LinkedIn. Growing your follower count no longer guarantees reach. The 3 signals that predict reach in 2026 are topic relevance, content quality signals, and engagement depth (likes alone are no longer sufficient).

## Post Format Distribution (2026 data, 1.3M posts)

| Format | Share of all posts |
|--------|--------------------|
| Text + Image | 57% |
| Text only | 12% |
| Video | 11% |
| Long-form articles (incl. Newsletters) | 6% |
| Instant Repost | 5% |
| Documents / Carousels | 4% |

**Note**: Distribution share does not equal performance. Carousels represent only 4% of posts but historically over-index on engagement and reach per impression. The 2024 report showed documents/carousels as the highest-performing format for reach despite low adoption.

## 6 Myths the Data Killed

From the 2026 report — these are "obvious" beliefs that turned out to be wrong or outdated:

1. **"Posting every day grows your account faster"** — False in 2026
2. **"More hashtags give you more reach"** — False
3. **"Mainly your followers see your posts"** — False (only 10% of initial distribution now)
4. **"Likes are still a weak engagement signal"** — False (reclassified)
5. **"Small accounts can't compete with big creators"** — False (Interest Graph levels the field)
6. **"Viral posts generate the most revenue"** — False

These are 6 of 20 myths covered in the full report.

## Implications for B2B SaaS Content Strategy

1. **Pick a lane and stay in it.** The Interest Graph rewards topical consistency over variety. A B2B SaaS founder posting about hiring, product, fundraising, AND industry trends will get diluted distribution. Pick 2–3 topics maximum.

2. **Stop optimizing for connections.** Connection request acceptance rates, network growth, "let's connect" messages — none of it moves the needle in 2026. Optimize for content quality in your topic cluster instead.

3. **Experiment with carousels/documents.** Only 4% of posts use this format, but the data shows they outperform on reach. Low competition, high reward.

4. **Engagement depth > engagement volume.** Comments and saves signal more than likes. Content that generates conversation (questions, contrarian takes, data reveals) will outperform content that generates passive agreement.

5. **Small accounts can win.** The Interest Graph means a 500-follower account posting sharp, niche content about ATS implementation will reach more of the right people than a 50K-follower account posting generic B2B advice.
