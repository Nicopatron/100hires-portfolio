## 8 themes across the playbook

### 1. Topic focus — the strongest point of agreement

**All 10 experts agree:** niche clarity outperforms broad reach in 2026.

| Expert | Position |
|---|---|
| Justin Welsh | "Be the guy who solves a very specific problem" — solopreneurship, not productivity |
| Adam Robinson | "Focus means making a few choices so aggressive that everything else becomes obvious" |
| Lara Acosta | "Hyper-specific content: solve one exact problem per post" |
| Richard van der Blom | Algorithm data: 10% of distribution is to connections, 50% via Interest Graph — topic cluster decides reach |
| Devin Reed | Mission statement unchanged for years: "Create the most engaging sales content" |
| Liam Moroney | "Win the underserved, not the undiscovered" |
| Amanda Natividad | Audience research yields specificity, templates don't |
| Steffen Hedebrandt | Narrow data niche (B2B attribution benchmarks) drives LinkedIn authority |
| Dave Gerhardt | Exit Five owns one niche (B2B marketing leaders) — everything flows from that |
| Ross Simmonds | Distribute one thing 50 times before creating new — requires a tight thesis |

**No disagreement detected.** The only tactical divergence: *how narrow* should the niche be (Welsh: extremely; Gerhardt: narrow-but-roomy).

**Implication**: pick one topic, stay for ≥12 months.

### 2. Cadence — agreement with one outlier

| Expert | Recommended cadence |
|---|---|
| Justin Welsh | ~2 posts/day (but he's at scale — maintenance, not growth) |
| Adam Robinson | "Me posting 3x/week" in the RB2B experiment setup |
| Lara Acosta | "3x a week" for hyper-specific content |
| Richard van der Blom | **DATA: "posting every day grows your account faster" is FALSE in 2026.** Quality + consistency > volume |
| Devin Reed | Consistency across years, not posts per week |
| Steffen Hedebrandt | Not prescriptive on cadence, but his own is ~2–3/week |
| Others | Not explicitly prescriptive |

**Agreement**: 3–5 high-signal posts per week beats daily posting of mixed quality.

**Outlier**: Welsh posts 2x/day but his context is (a) a mature ~1M+ audience, (b) a solopreneur content-as-product business, (c) content IS the business. Not transferable for a bootstrapped B2B SaaS founder with 35 other jobs.

**Implication**: **3 posts/week** is the defensible starting cadence. Quality bar per post.

### 3. Hooks and post structure

| Expert | Position |
|---|---|
| Adam Robinson | "Hook, Hook, Hook. If you don't get people to click 'see more' in the first line, nothing else matters." |
| Lara Acosta | 80% tactical frameworks + 20% stories |
| Richard van der Blom | Format matters less than topic signals — but **carousels** over-index (4% of posts, disproportionate reach) |
| Adam Robinson | "Edu-tainment: every post needs a lesson wrapped in a dopamine hit" |
| Justin Welsh | Hook → body → call-to-conversation → optional link to article |
| Devin Reed | "Open with curiosity, not a product description. 8x differences in performance based on the first two sentences alone" |

**Agreement**: opening 1–2 lines determine read-through. Every post needs a payoff.

**Tension**: Adam ("Story > Everything") vs. Lara ("80% tactical"). **Resolution**: both are right depending on stage. Pre-authority (Lara's context — growing from 0): lead with frameworks to establish trust. Post-authority (Adam's context — $8M ARR with known name): stories amplify. For 100Hires starting from 256 followers, the Lara side.

**Implication**: carousels + tactical-heavy posts to build authority; stories layer in after 6 months.

### 4. Audience research — the overlooked prerequisite

| Expert | Position |
|---|---|
| Amanda Natividad | "Templates don't give you taste. Audience research does." Owns this theme. |
| Liam Moroney | Customer research is how you avoid expensive assumptions about what buyers want |
| Justin Welsh | Multi-step: practical experience + learned obsession + "what do people come to you for?" + problems you've solved yourself |
| Devin Reed | Content for the 95% not in-market = requires knowing what that 95% cares about beyond your product |
| All others | Implicit — everyone agrees, no one disagrees |

**Agreement**: research the audience before writing. Natividad's 5-minute method (read 5 ICP profiles, copy exact language) is the lowest-cost starting point.

**No disagreement.** This is under-practiced, not under-agreed.

**Implication**: day-1 action for any Growth hire — 5-minute audience research × 10 ICP profiles before the first content calendar.

### 5. Measurement and ROI — nuanced agreement

The apparent disagreement (attribution vs. brand metrics) resolves to: **attribution is insufficient but not useless.**

| Expert | Position |
|---|---|
| Steffen Hedebrandt | Attribution works IF it's account-based, multi-touch, and spans the full 272-day journey. 81% of the B2B journey happens pre-pipeline. LinkedIn ROAS: 121% (vs. 67% Google Search, 51% Meta) |
| Liam Moroney | "Brand is measurable. It's the leadership understanding that's lacking." Attribution tools miss the underlying conditions. |
| Amanda Natividad | "Attribution tries to assign credit. Measurement tries to understand impact. Attribution dashboards see ~24% of visits; the other ~76% is invisible." |
| Dave Gerhardt | "Measurement should meet the marketing where it is" — use incrementality testing for brand work, not last-click |
| Devin Reed | Executive conversation should be revenue-adjacent, not CTR-adjacent |

**Consensus**: standard last-touch attribution is wrong for B2B. The path forward:
1. Measure company page visits as a revenue signal (Hedebrandt's specific finding: 17.9% of new business involves a LinkedIn company page visit)
2. Use incrementality tests for brand investments (Gerhardt)
3. Accept that ~75% of what works is invisible to the CRM (Natividad, Hedebrandt)

**Implication**: 100Hires already has programmatic SEO attribution. LinkedIn needs a different measurement stack: company page visit tracking + incrementality testing + 272-day pipeline lag modeling.

### 6. Brand vs. performance — agreement against "performance-only"

| Expert | Position |
|---|---|
| Liam Moroney | 80–90% of deals go to day-one consideration sets. Brand is the conditions, not the tactic. |
| Devin Reed | 95% of buyers aren't in-market now — brand plays are the only way to be top-of-mind when they are |
| Steffen Hedebrandt | Hard data: LinkedIn company pages show up in 17.9% of won deals. Brand surfaces ARE revenue surfaces. |
| Amanda Natividad | Zero-click marketing — earn attention on its own terms, not via click conversion |
| Dave Gerhardt | Community + brand > lead capture. NPS 89 at Exit Five events. |
| Justin Welsh | Own the distribution channel — the channel IS the brand |

**No expert argues for pure performance marketing.** Disagreement is only on emphasis (% of time/budget).

**Implication for 100Hires**: the current strategy (programmatic SEO + review platforms) is performance-heavy. LinkedIn organic is the brand layer that makes both work better. Moroney's frame: SEO captures intent, but only for brands in the day-one consideration set — which is what LinkedIn content builds.

### 7. Distribution — the most under-executed piece

| Expert | Position |
|---|---|

## Identified gap: Organic → Pipeline handoff

**None of the 10 experts cover operationally** how a LinkedIn-generated impression becomes a pipeline opportunity for a self-serve/SMB SaaS like 100Hires.

What they DO cover:
- Hedebrandt: the 272-day journey exists (descriptive, not prescriptive)
- Reed: Gong sales reps "use our content in their calls" (mentioned, not detailed)
- Robinson: RB2B traffic → signup (only possible because RB2B's self-serve motion is instant)

What's missing:
- **Trigger mechanics**: which LinkedIn signals (post engagement, company page visit, newsletter subscription, comment reply) are worth routing to sales outreach?
- **Handoff cadence**: if someone engages with a post, when do you reach out, and how does that outreach avoid feeling creepy-tracked?
- **Self-serve interrupt**: 100Hires has 14-day trial. What's the workflow for a LinkedIn impression to become a trial signup without an SDR in between?
- **SMB-specific patterns**: enterprise ABM (Hedebrandt's wheelhouse) and solopreneur self-serve (Welsh's wheelhouse) both work. The SMB middle — 5–50 person buying teams, $75–$400/mo price point — has no documented playbook from this cohort.

**Why the gap exists**: the practitioners who've documented LinkedIn-to-pipeline operations work for enterprise (Gong, Dreamdata) or pure product-led solopreneurs (Welsh). The SMB-ATS case is specific enough that the handoff logic has to be built, not copied.

**Step 3 addresses this** as a dedicated section. It will draw on Hedebrandt's intent-signal framework, Robinson's founder-led attribution patterns, and Natividad's audience research to design a handoff that fits a bootstrapped 11-person team selling $75–$400/mo to HR leads at SMBs.

## Implications for a 100Hires playbook (preview)

This section seeds Step 3. Each line is directly supported by the synthesis above.

1. **One topic, 12+ months**: "ATS and hiring operations for founders and HR leads at 10–100 person companies." Specific enough to matter, broad enough to sustain content. (Welsh, Acosta, Moroney, Reed)
2. **Alex as founder-led voice, Growth hire as distribution engine**: hybrid model. (Robinson, Welsh)
3. **3 posts/week, 80% tactical, lead with carousels**: cadence and format arbitrage. (Acosta, Van der Blom)
4. **Audience research first**: 5-minute method on 20 ICP profiles before the first content calendar. (Natividad)
5. **Distribution-first of existing assets**: blog, reviews, comparison pages × 30 formats. Reddit + YouTube included. (Simmonds)
6. **Company page visits as revenue signal**: instrument and retarget. (Hedebrandt)
7. **12-month mission discipline**: no format pivots for a full year. (Reed)
8. **Measurement stack**: company-page visits + incrementality tests + 272-day lag modeling. Not last-touch. (Hedebrandt, Gerhardt, Moroney, Natividad)
9. **Brand investment justified by 81% pre-pipeline data**: the CFO conversation uses Hedebrandt's numbers. (Hedebrandt, Moroney)
10. **Pipeline handoff workflow**: custom-designed for SMB self-serve. This is Step 3's novel contribution.
