## Post 6 — 2026-04-06

**Engagement**: 305 reactions | 144 comments

Every year, LinkedIn tears itself apart over something new.
And every year, the same pattern repeats.

2020 — Selfies and personal stories
"This isn't Facebook." The backlash against vulnerability content was loud. Until it became the norm.

2021 — Creator Mode
Personal branding as a growth path. Some called it vanity. Others built empires.

2022 — Link vs. no-link posts
The awakening. People finally realised the algorithm wasn't neutral.

2023 — Engagement pods
Gamifying visibility when organic reach dropped. Praised by some. Despised by many.

2024 — Video content boom
LinkedIn trying to keep pace with TikTok and Reels culture. Creators split on whether it belonged here.

2025 — AI-written posts
Efficiency vs. authenticity. Still unresolved.

2026 — Engagement bait tactics
"Comment to get X." Automation of authenticity at scale.

Each divide isn't random.
It reflects the same tension: visibility vs. authenticity vs. control.

The cycle stays the same:
1. A new feature or behaviour emerges
2. Early adopters exploit it
3. The community polarises
4. LinkedIn adjusts the rules

The creators who win aren't the ones who pick the "right" side.
They're the ones who understand why each controversy happens — and move before the rules change.
