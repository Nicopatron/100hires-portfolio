## Post 4 — 2026-03-26

**Engagement**: 344 reactions | 29 comments

THE B2B JOURNEY CONTINUES TO MOVE OUTSIDE THE SALES PIPELINE

81% of the B2B customer journey now happens before the sales pipeline.

A year ago, it was 70%.

That shift reflects how quickly buyer behavior is changing.

Today, B2B buyers spend months researching, comparing solutions, and forming opinions before they ever speak to sales.

In our data, the average journey spans 272 days - with the majority of that time happening outside the pipeline.

For marketers, this changes where impact is created.

A few things become more important:
- Showing up early — before there is active demand
- Investing in content that educates, not just converts
- Building brand familiarity over time, not just capturing intent
- Creating multiple touchpoints across channels, not relying on single campaigns
- Understanding how early engagement connects to pipeline later on

Because by the time a deal enters the CRM's Sales Pipeline, much of the decision has already been shaped.

The challenge is that this part of the journey is still hard to see for most companies.

Most traditional reporting focuses on what happens after a lead is created - not the months of activity that led up to it.

## Post 5 — 2026-03-19

**Engagement**: 143 reactions | 25 comments

THIS LINKEDIN BENCHMARK WAS ONE OF THE BIGGEST EYE-OPENERS

Your LinkedIn company page might be doing more for revenue than you think.

We've been looking at some new benchmarks on how LinkedIn company page activity shows up in B2B buying journeys and honestly, I did not expect this:

- 9.4% of MQLs
- 15.0% of SQLs (Sales Pipeline)
- 17.9% of New Business

…had a LinkedIn company page impression as part of the journey.

And here is the interesting part:
- In ~18% of won deals, buyers visited the company page before buying
- On average, it takes ~90 days from that visit to becoming a customer

This means: Visitors on your company's LinkedIn page is not just "brand awareness". It is actually a revenue signal.

To me, this opens up some very real Go-to-market use cases:
- Using company page visits as intent data for ads
- Using it to inform Sales Navigator outreach
- Thinking more deliberately about how organic posts drive people to the company page

We have all known LinkedIn matters. Now we can actually measure how much it matters.
