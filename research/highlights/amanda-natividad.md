## 1. Templates don't give you taste — audience research does

> "We marketers are obsessed with frameworks and templates. But templates don't give you taste. Audience research does. Spend an hour reading what your audience actually posts (LinkedIn comments, Subreddit threads, Slack communities). You'll quickly see what excites them, what annoys them, and what language they use to describe it. That's how you build taste."

- **Source**: [Post 6, 2025-10-02](../linkedin-posts/amanda-natividad.md) (169 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: The prerequisite step most LinkedIn playbooks skip. Before the hook templates, before the content calendar: one hour of reading your ICP's language. Concrete, measurable, actionable from day one.

## 2. The 5-minute audience research method

> "Here's a quick way to uncover insights: 1) Pick 5 customers or prospects you already know. 2) Go to their LinkedIn. Read their headlines and About sections. 3) Copy/paste the exact words they use to describe themselves, their role, or their goals. 4) Compare across all 5. What words or phrases repeat? Those phrases give you copy that will feel instantly familiar to them."

- **Source**: [Post 8, 2025-08-19](../linkedin-posts/amanda-natividad.md)
- **Why it matters**: The least-cost, highest-ROI research method. A new Growth hire at 100Hires can do this on day one across 5 HR leaders and have a usable messaging document by end of day.

## 3. Earn attention by being worth paying attention to

> "Zero Click Marketing doesn't feel like a new idea to me. It's a name for something I'd already been doing for years. It's about making things people actually want to consume. Things that are useful or interesting on their own. That don't need a click to justify their existence... Earn attention by being worth paying attention to."

- **Source**: [Post 4, 2026-03-27](../linkedin-posts/amanda-natividad.md) (341 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: The anti-clickbait principle. For B2B LinkedIn in 2026, posts that deliver value in the feed outperform posts that gate value behind a click. Content philosophy shift: publish the answer, not the teaser.

## 4. Attribution ≠ measurement

> "Attribution tries to assign credit. Measurement tries to understand impact. And if you're only doing the first one, you're not really measuring your marketing... Search accounts for about 24% of visits to the top 5,000 websites. The other ~76% is where attention actually lives — social, news, email, forums, communities. Your attribution dashboard sees none of it."

- **Source**: [Post 2, 2026-04-07](../linkedin-posts/amanda-natividad.md) (73 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: Distinguishes two different jobs that teams conflate. When a CMO defunds brand because "search converts better," they're defunding the activities that made search work. Pairs directly with Hedebrandt (81% of journey is invisible) and Moroney (brand is measurable, not via attribution).

## 5. Audience research > keyword research

> "My most successful content marketing strategy had nothing to do with keyword research. At Fitbit... we published white papers that drove thousands of leads, wrote case studies that closed deals, planned conference agendas competitors outright copied. We weren't trying to rank for 'what is corporate wellness?' Because no decision-maker worth selling to was Googling that. Instead, we drove influence."

- **Source**: [Post 7, 2025-09-30](../linkedin-posts/amanda-natividad.md) (270 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: Case study against the dominant SEO-first B2B content playbook. For 100Hires with existing programmatic SEO strength, this is where LinkedIn organic complements: influence channels that SEO can't touch.

## 6. Zero click doesn't mean zero sales

> "Zero Click Marketing does NOT mean zero sales. People still need to buy things... What changes is how they decide who to buy from."

- **Source**: [Post 5, 2026-03-12](../linkedin-posts/amanda-natividad.md)
- **Why it matters**: Removes the "but we need leads" objection to in-feed content. Demand still exists; the purchase path just shifts. A team obsessing over form-fills is missing where decisions are actually formed.

## 7. The internet is writing your brand's public record

> "Your brand's public record is being written without you... It's happening in AI answers, Reddit threads, reviews, comparison pages. Audit the current public record, treat third-party surfaces like part of your brand footprint, identify the weak claims that are winning, publish better facts that only you can publish."

- **Source**: [Post 1, 2026-04-14](../linkedin-posts/amanda-natividad.md) (90 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: Operational framework for the modern brand stack. For 100Hires: the G2/Capterra reviews, comparison articles, and AI citation footprint are the brand. LinkedIn content is how you publish "better facts only you can publish" to shape the record.
