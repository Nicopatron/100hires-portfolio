## 1. 81% of the B2B journey happens before the sales pipeline

> "81% of the B2B customer journey now happens before the sales pipeline. A year ago, it was 70%. In our data, the average journey spans 272 days — with the majority of that time happening outside the pipeline. By the time a deal enters the CRM's Sales Pipeline, much of the decision has already been shaped."

- **Source**: [Post 4, 2026-03-26](../linkedin-posts/steffen-hedebrandt.md) (344 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: The hard number behind everything Liam Moroney argues theoretically. If 81% of the journey is invisible to your CRM, your attribution is watching the wrong 19%. Reframes the entire "pipeline-first" mindset.

## 2. 272 days means brand investments today pay back in three quarters

> "If the average customer journey is 272 days, there's a limit to how many times you can say 'book a demo'. Most of your market isn't ready to buy. But they're still paying attention... So when something does happen, you're already the one they think of."

- **Source**: [Post 2, 2026-04-09](../linkedin-posts/steffen-hedebrandt.md)
- **Why it matters**: Converts brand investment from faith-based to math-based. A CFO can model 272-day lag explicitly. For a bootstrapped team: content published in Q2 compounds into Q4/Q1 pipeline, which affects budget decisions today.

## 3. LinkedIn ROAS: 121% vs. Google Search 67%, Meta 51%

> "We're seeing ~121% ROAS on LinkedIn. It uses humor without overdoing it. It still connects clearly to what we actually offer: real benchmarks for B2B marketers... If you show numbers like 121% ROAS, you need to back them up."

- **Source**: [Post 6, 2026-03-19](../linkedin-posts/steffen-hedebrandt.md) + Dreamdata LinkedIn Ads Benchmark Report 2026
- **Why it matters**: The single cleanest attribution data supporting LinkedIn as a B2B channel over alternatives. Ends the "LinkedIn is too expensive" objection with comparable data from 66M sessions.

## 4. LinkedIn company page visits are revenue signals, not brand awareness

> "9.4% of MQLs, 15.0% of SQLs (Sales Pipeline), 17.9% of New Business had a LinkedIn company page impression as part of the journey. In ~18% of won deals, buyers visited the company page before buying. Average ~90 days from that visit to becoming a customer. Company page visits are not just brand awareness. It is a revenue signal."

- **Source**: [Post 5, 2026-03-19](../linkedin-posts/steffen-hedebrandt.md) (143 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: Actionable measurement tactic. A Growth team can instrument company page visits as a leading indicator, route them to Sales Navigator, and build ad retargeting off the signal. No other B2B source quantifies this as clearly.

## 5. Track account-level journeys, not individual contact journeys

> "In B2B then you're typically trying to sell to a buying committee of three four or five people... The ones that typically start the journeys and the ones that are being affected by your marketing spend they're very rarely the guys that signs the contracts five six seven months later."

- **Source**: [transcript: How to Attribute B2B Marketing Activities to Revenue](../youtube-transcripts/steffen-hedebrandt--how-to-attribute-b2b-marketing-activities-to-revenue.md)
- **Why it matters**: Ends the flawed single-lead attribution model. Growth teams measuring per-contact miss that the demo-booker and the contract-signer are different people. 100Hires sells into buying committees (HR + hiring manager + ops).

## 6. Real customer journeys are ~1.5x longer than you think

> "90% of what people reply if you ask them about how long is your customer Journeys they're actually replying from the time that they got a lead until you won the account. What we can see in our data is that the length of time people spend Anonymous researching is typically one or one X similar to the links that they spent being identified as an account. So if you say your sales cycle is three months it's probably more like six months seven months."

- **Source**: [transcript: How to Attribute B2B Marketing Activities to Revenue](../youtube-transcripts/steffen-hedebrandt--how-to-attribute-b2b-marketing-activities-to-revenue.md)
- **Why it matters**: Forces budget planning to back up further. To hit Q3 pipeline, content investment must be in Q1 — not Q2 as most teams assume.
