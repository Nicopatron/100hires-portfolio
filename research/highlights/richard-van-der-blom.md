## 1. Your connections no longer determine your reach

> "In 2024, 70% of your post's initial distribution went to your connections. In 2025, that dropped to 54%. In 2026? Just 10%. Meanwhile, the Interest Graph went from 5% → 21% → 50%. LinkedIn completely flipped the distribution model."

- **Source**: [Post 3, 2026-04-14](../linkedin-posts/richard-van-der-blom.md) (1,371 reactions, 456 comments)
- **Why it matters**: This one stat invalidates most pre-2025 LinkedIn playbooks. Any strategy anchored in "grow your network" needs to be rebuilt around topic clusters.

## 2. The algorithm now asks: "What does this person stand for?"

> "Your post doesn't travel through your network first. It travels through your topic cluster. The algorithm now asks: 'What does this person stand for?' Not: 'Who does this person know?' Your niche clarity matters more than your network size."

- **Source**: [Post 3, 2026-04-14](../linkedin-posts/richard-van-der-blom.md)
- **Why it matters**: Strategic principle behind tactical advice. Explains why narrow-topic creators outperform high-follower generalists. For 100Hires: pick 2-3 topics and drill, don't sprawl.

## 3. The follower paradox: growing accounts, shrinking reach

> "Follower counts grew 31%. Impressions fell. Not optimization. It's a deliberate architectural decision. The 3 signals that predict reach in 2026 are topic relevance, content quality signals, and engagement depth (likes aren't one of them)."

- **Source**: [Post 4, 2026-04-12](../linkedin-posts/richard-van-der-blom.md) (388 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: Vanity metric (followers) is decoupled from distribution. Removes the "we need 10K followers first" excuse. Small accounts with niche depth out-reach large accounts with generic feeds.

## 4. Format distribution vs. format performance

> "Text + Image: 57% of all posts. Text only: 12%. Video: 11%. Long-form articles: 6%. Instant Repost: 5%. Documents/Carousels: 4%. Documents are only 4% of posts but historically over-index on engagement and reach per impression."

- **Source**: [Post 5, 2026-04-08](../linkedin-posts/richard-van-der-blom.md) + [algorithm findings](../other/linkedin-algorithm-2024-2026-key-findings.md)
- **Why it matters**: The arbitrage opportunity. Low-competition format (carousels) with historically highest reach. For a Growth team starting: lead with carousels before text+image is the default.

## 5. Six "obvious" LinkedIn myths the data killed

> "1. Posting every day grows your account faster — FALSE in 2026. 2. More hashtags give you more reach — FALSE. 3. Mainly your followers see your posts — FALSE (only 10%). 4. Likes are still a weak engagement signal — FALSE (reclassified). 5. Small accounts can't compete with big creators — FALSE. 6. Viral posts generate the most revenue — FALSE."

- **Source**: [Post 1, 2026-04-16](../linkedin-posts/richard-van-der-blom.md) (440 reactions) + [algorithm findings](../other/linkedin-algorithm-2024-2026-key-findings.md)
- **Why it matters**: These six reverse common LinkedIn advice. Any playbook that says "post daily" or "use 3-5 hashtags" is built on pre-2026 premises. Essential correction layer for the whole synthesis.

## 6. AI-assisted content tools help accounts that already have a practice

> "If you post once a week when you feel like it? If content and consistency isn't your thing? Stanley won't help you. It's built for people whose business depends on visibility... on showing up with value — consistently. Posts created with Stanley support: 1.8x more engagement."

- **Source**: [Post 2, 2026-04-15](../linkedin-posts/richard-van-der-blom.md) (102 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: Honest framing on AI content tools. Amplifier, not a replacement. For 100Hires: AI helps if the founder/team is already posting; it doesn't start the practice.
