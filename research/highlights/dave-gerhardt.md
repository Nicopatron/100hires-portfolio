## 1. Measurement should meet the marketing where it is

> "Love this soundbite here: 'MEASUREMENT SHOULD MEET THE MARKETING WHERE IT IS.' Drew breaks down how Ramp uses incrementality testing and event studies to put real numbers on brand stunts... split budget 70/30 between proven channels and experimental bets."

- **Source**: [Post 2, 2026-04-14](../linkedin-posts/dave-gerhardt.md) (112 reactions, quoting Drew Pinta at Ramp)
- **Why it matters**: Inverts the default "measure everything or don't do it." Instead: pick a measurement approach fit for the marketing type. Brand stunts get incrementality tests, not first-touch attribution. Pairs with Moroney's "thermometer for climate change" argument.

## 2. The 70/30 budget split: proven vs. experimental

> "Drew breaks down how Ramp... split budget 70/30 between proven channels and experimental bets."

- **Source**: [Post 2, 2026-04-14](../linkedin-posts/dave-gerhardt.md)
- **Why it matters**: Concrete budget framework. 30% protects experimentation without betting the whole quarter on unproven channels. For a small Growth team, keeps a fixed line item for new formats (video, carousels) without destabilizing core performance.

## 3. In-person is a force multiplier for the online community

> "We have this online community (Exit Five) some percentages of event attendees feel like they already know each other, and even if that's only 25% of the room it makes a big, big difference... in-person is an INCREDIBLE force multiplier for community. NPS score from this event was 89."

- **Source**: [Post 6, 2026-03-30](../linkedin-posts/dave-gerhardt.md) (161 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: The path from LinkedIn organic → community → events → lifetime loyalty. For a bootstrapped team not ready for conferences yet, LinkedIn organic builds the community layer first; IRL comes later and multiplies retention.

## 4. Work with people who make things simple

> "Smart people find simple solutions. They don't try to sound smart. They make things easy to understand. They are not sending you in circles with numbers and metrics and dashboards and 'business theater' that leaves you feeling more confused than when you started."

- **Source**: [Post 7, 2026-03-30](../linkedin-posts/dave-gerhardt.md) (266 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: Voice principle. The 100Hires brand is "simple vs. bloated enterprise" — Dave's writing bar reinforces the brand. Posts that confuse should not ship. Clear > clever, always.

## 5. LinkedIn content generates OOH-scale impressions for free

> "Big news out of Exit Five HQ. For the first time we're dabbling in out of home advertising... No one in the field will have any idea what Exit Five does. They probably don't work in B2B marketing. But every now and then you get to pull the Founder Card and run advertising plays how you want. I also know that I'll get more than enough impressions from posting about this on LinkedIn alone to justify the ROI."

- **Source**: [Post 5, 2026-03-31](../linkedin-posts/dave-gerhardt.md)
- **Why it matters**: The OOH cost is justified by the LinkedIn post about the OOH. The organic channel is the actual scale layer. Inverts the usual relationship between paid and organic.

## 6. CMO community exists because it's lonely at the top

> "CMOs always tell us it's lonely at the top. You'd think that when you climb the ranks, you have all the support needed to do your job successfully, but it's actually the opposite... This is why peer communities like the Exit Five CMO Council exist."

- **Source**: [Post 1, 2026-04-15](../linkedin-posts/dave-gerhardt.md)
- **Why it matters**: Insight into the buyer psychology. HR leaders (100Hires buyers) face the same loneliness — content that names the lonely experience ("the only person in the company who cares about TAT") resonates because no one else has named it.
