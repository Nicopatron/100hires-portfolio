## 1. Distribute one piece 50 times before creating the next

> "Normalize distributing one piece of content 50 times before creating a new one. Everyone's obsessed with 'more content.' Your best post from 8 months ago hasn't been seen by 95% of your audience... Repost it. I beg you."

- **Source**: [Post 1, 2026-04-12](../linkedin-posts/ross-simmonds.md)
- **Why it matters**: The core content distribution principle in one sentence. Removes the content-factory burnout that kills most Growth functions. 100Hires already has high-value content (comparison pages, blog) that's under-distributed.

## 2. One exceptional piece × 30 distributions > 30 pieces × 1 distribution each

> "I'm convinced that some companies have already created all the content they need for 2026... Most companies create too much content and distribute too little of it. The content calendar is full. The blog has 300 posts. The YouTube channel has 200 videos. And nobody has seen any of it. One exceptional piece of content distributed 30 different ways will outperform 30 pieces of content distributed once. Stop feeding the content machine. Start feeding the distribution engine. The ROI isn't in the creation. It's in the repetition."

- **Source**: [Post 4, 2026-04-09](../linkedin-posts/ross-simmonds.md) (59 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: Direct critique of the default B2B content budget allocation (90% creation, 10% distribution). For a small team at 100Hires, this flips the math: pick 10 pillar pieces, distribute each 30 ways. Still 300 outputs, 1/10th the creation cost.

## 3. Reddit owns bottom-of-funnel SaaS buying

> "Reddit dominates buyer-intent 'best software' queries. Meanwhile: YouTube is now cited in a huge share of LLM answers. Stop posting demos. Start publishing problem-solving content where buyers already ask questions. Someone just made a $1M decision because a Reddit thread told them they should avoid this software and instead buy that software. Reddit owns the bottom of funnel in the SERP and across LLMs. Don't sleep on it."

- **Source**: [Post 2, 2026-04-11 + Post 5, 2026-04-08](../linkedin-posts/ross-simmonds.md)
- **Why it matters**: For 100Hires, r/humanresources, r/recruiting, r/talentacquisition are where SMB buyers compare ATS options. Showing up there (as the founder, not as a brand shill) could single-handedly shift purchase momentum. Largely unworked channel.

## 4. AI visibility compounds — build inventory now

> "The best time to build AI visibility was 10 years ago. The second best time is now. If you spent the last decade creating useful content, earning reviews, showing up on Reddit, publishing on YouTube, and getting mentioned offsite... you're getting cited today. If not, start stacking distribution now. Every month you wait, the gap compounds."

- **Source**: [Post 3, 2026-04-10](../linkedin-posts/ross-simmonds.md) (75 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: The argument for investing in LinkedIn + distribution channels isn't just 2026 pipeline — it's 2028 AI citation inventory. Each piece of content compounds in LLM training/retrieval corpora. Delay is expensive.

## 5. Brands ignore the goldmine they already have

> "Most brands are sitting on a goldmine... and ignoring it. Years of YouTube videos, blog posts, podcasts, webinars — all collecting dust after pressing publish. But AI makes this scalable. Here's where most brands get it wrong... They use AI to create more noise. Instead of AI to distribute stories that already resonate and shape culture."

- **Source**: [Post 6, 2026-04-07](../linkedin-posts/ross-simmonds.md)
- **Why it matters**: Specifically for 100Hires: the blog (Alex's writing, Olivia's content) and the 943 Capterra reviews are under-repurposed. Before creating new, AI-assist redistribution of what exists. Lower risk, higher ROI.

## 6. Don't treat Reddit and YouTube like side projects

> "Most B2B teams treat Reddit and YouTube like side projects. Meanwhile: Reddit dominates buyer-intent 'best software' queries. YouTube is now cited in a huge share of LLM answers. Consistency wins citations."

- **Source**: [Post 2, 2026-04-11](../linkedin-posts/ross-simmonds.md)
- **Why it matters**: Direct call to include Reddit + YouTube in the core distribution plan, not as "nice-to-have." 100Hires has no YouTube presence — a weekly 5-minute Alex teardown of a hiring workflow would build inventory fast.
