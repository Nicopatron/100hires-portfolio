## 1. 80-90% of deals go to day-one consideration sets

> "Research continues to show that 80-90% of the time the deal will go to familiar brands who were day one considerations. Anyone not in the memory bank before that customer journey starts is massively disadvantaged."

- **Source**: [Post 3, 2026-03-10](../linkedin-posts/liam-moroney.md)
- **Why it matters**: The theoretical anchor for why organic content compounds. If the deal is mostly won before the buyer enters the pipeline, the point of LinkedIn content is to be in the memory bank before the buying journey begins — not to capture leads today.

## 2. The marketing journey is non-linear — the customer journey is linear

> "The customer journey and the marketing journey are not the same thing... When we decide to buy something, there's a certain linearity to the effort... The marketing journey, however, is not linear, and growth efforts can be tremendously harmed when it's made to be. For most purchases, we don't only begin to learn about the brands once we've decided that we need to buy something."

- **Source**: [Post 3, 2026-03-10](../linkedin-posts/liam-moroney.md) (117 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: Distinguishes "buying" from "branding." Funnels/pipelines are for the buying journey. The LinkedIn organic playbook lives in the non-linear marketing journey. Conflating them kills brand investment.

## 3. Win the underserved, not the undiscovered

> "Blank slate buyers who are problem aware but not solution aware are a fringe group, if they even exist at all. Most of our addressable market knows and has experience with the category, and have some form of a consideration set in mind. The job in marketing isn't to find the undiscovered buyers, but to win the underserved ones."

- **Source**: [Post 5, 2026-03-04](../linkedin-posts/liam-moroney.md)
- **Why it matters**: Reframes content strategy. For 100Hires, the prospects already know Greenhouse, Lever, Workable. The job is displacement (underserved), not education (undiscovered). Changes what the LinkedIn content should say.

## 4. Brand is measurable — leadership understanding is what's missing

> "Brand gets underinvested in because it doesn't show up clearly enough in our attribution models, but should it? Brand is measurable... It's the leadership understanding that's lacking. When we force it to show up in a measurement tool designed for something else entirely, we set it up to fail. It's like trying to measure climate change with a thermometer."

- **Source**: [Post 4, 2026-03-05](../linkedin-posts/liam-moroney.md)
- **Why it matters**: Gives a Growth hire a framework for the inevitable "what's the ROI on this LinkedIn post" conversation with leadership. Paired with Hedebrandt's 121% ROAS data, this moves from abstract defense to empirical grounding.

## 5. AI visibility claims are overblown

> "'If AI can't find you, your buyer won't either' — these are baseless claims and totally misunderstand how people buy complex consideration purchases. How likely is it that all of that changed overnight, where buyers now cede all vendor choices to AI? Yes, AI is being leveraged in most buying journeys now but that's fundamentally different to it being the foundation of who makes it into the consideration set."

- **Source**: [Post 1, 2026-04-09](../linkedin-posts/liam-moroney.md)
- **Why it matters**: Rare data-driven skepticism of the "optimize for AI search" trend. Saves a new Growth hire from chasing the wrong priority. Buying behavior hasn't changed overnight just because ChatGPT cites sources.

## 6. Word of mouth is a byproduct of brand, not a tactic

> "Word of mouth is not a tactic, but rather a byproduct of growth. The very reason that WOM is valuable is because it's an authentic reflection of your brand delivering value... Word of mouth is ultimately tied to how many customers we have, how big our market share is, and how much awareness we have through brand marketing. It's the reason to invest in brand, because you cannot be talked about if you are not known."

- **Source**: [Post 6, 2026-02-27](../linkedin-posts/liam-moroney.md)
- **Why it matters**: Prevents the common mistake of trying to "operationalize WOM" with referral programs. For 100Hires with 943 Capterra reviews at 4.9 — WOM is already working because the brand+product is. The lever is more brand investment, not more referral mechanics.
