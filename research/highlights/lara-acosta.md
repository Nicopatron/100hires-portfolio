## 1. Hyper-specific beats broad reach

> "Hyper-specific content (3x a week): solve one exact problem per post, use industry language your clients use, speak directly to one person. Jake Ward has mastered this for B2B: 'Stop losing credibility with your body language.'"

- **Source**: [Post 1, 2026-04-13](../linkedin-posts/lara-acosta.md) (2,232 reactions, 753 comments)
- **Why it matters**: Most B2B founders try to speak to "everyone in tech." Lara's data-backed guidance: speak to exactly one person, one problem. Compounds with Van der Blom's Interest Graph data.

## 2. The 80/20 content ratio: tactical frameworks vs. narrative

> "80% tactical step-by-step frameworks and screenshots. 20% stories on how you're doing it. The 80% builds trust, the 20% engages. Educate and storytell. Always both."

- **Source**: [Post 1, 2026-04-13](../linkedin-posts/lara-acosta.md)
- **Why it matters**: Concrete operational ratio. Disagrees with Adam Robinson's "story > everything" — Lara argues frameworks are the trust anchor, stories are the engagement amplifier. Both work; the mix depends on stage.

## 3. Profile > posts for discoverability in 2026

> "Your profile is the most important asset in 2026. It's how LinkedIn's new AI puts you in front of the right people... Your profile shouldn't just say 'founder' or 'coach'. It needs to be obvious, clear and have an outcome."

- **Source**: [Post 4, 2026-03-31](../linkedin-posts/lara-acosta.md) (1,654 reactions, 763 comments)
- **Why it matters**: Posted after visiting LinkedIn HQ London. With Interest Graph distribution, profile becomes the topic classifier. Headline rewrite is higher ROI than a week of posting.

## 4. Comments are leverage when you have none

> "Find 20 to 30 active accounts in your niche or adjacent ones... 0 to 5K is usually people in the same boat as you and most likely to reciprocate... 5 to 20K are usually steps ahead... 20K+ maybe harder to stand out but if you do you get a double win... Comment as early as possible on their post."

- **Source**: [transcript: I Blew Up My LinkedIn Following As Fast As I Could](../youtube-transcripts/lara-acosta--i-blew-up-my-linkedin-following-as-fast-as-i-could.md)
- **Why it matters**: Concrete distribution mechanic for accounts starting from zero. The "tiered commenting ladder" (small/mid/large) is directly operable for a 100Hires account with <300 followers.

## 5. Three comment types that work — and three that don't

> "You don't leave 'great post' or '100% ask' comments. You don't use AI tools to comment. You don't use single emoji ass comments. You need to leave three types: a personal take on what the post was about, a motivational mic drop, or if you're like me a meme — use your humor."

- **Source**: [transcript: I Blew Up My LinkedIn Following As Fast As I Could](../youtube-transcripts/lara-acosta--i-blew-up-my-linkedin-following-as-fast-as-i-could.md)
- **Why it matters**: Actionable quality bar for engagement. Removes the single largest time-waster (low-value comments) from a Growth operator's day.

## 6. Repetition builds reputation

> "I'm allergic to shiny objects and cool ideas. Instead, I follow the boring basics and data. They repeat the same tasks. They keep building a boring business. They talk about the same thing on LinkedIn... Repetition builds reputation over time."

- **Source**: [Post 5, 2026-03-30](../linkedin-posts/lara-acosta.md) (1,186 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: The antidote to the "try every new format" trap. For a bootstrapped team, discipline of repeating 2-3 topics > exploring 10.
