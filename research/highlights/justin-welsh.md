## 1. Owning a distribution channel is the only moat that compounds

> "You need to own the assets that the company currently gives you: a product or service, a distribution channel. The easiest way to do this is to create your own distribution channel. I can singlehandedly create 100x more impressions for my business on social media than a typical startup. That's how important these channels are."

- **Source**: [Post 1, 2026-03-30](../linkedin-posts/justin-welsh.md) (2,531 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: Frames LinkedIn organic not as a marketing channel but as the founder's core asset. For a bootstrapped team, it's the one asset that doesn't evaporate with ad budget cuts.

## 2. Be interesting first, "build a brand" second

> "Building a 'personal brand' is so 2021. Instead, become a person worth paying attention to. Most people struggle to come up with good content because they're not actually doing anything interesting offline. In 2026, the people building interesting and exciting brands are actively studying something."

- **Source**: [Post 6, 2026-02-25](../linkedin-posts/justin-welsh.md) (1,902 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: Inverts the typical content-first playbook. Explains why most company LinkedIn content feels hollow — the operator isn't generating novel input offline.

## 3. Three protected hours on Monday produce everything downstream

> "The heart of my 8-figure business is a messy Google Doc. Every Monday, I sit down... and I just write. It takes about 3 hours. Everything else in my business exists because of that one thing. Most people fill their calendars with calls, meetings, and 'strategy,' and then fall asleep on the couch before they ever get to the work that matters."

- **Source**: [Post 3, 2026-03-02](../linkedin-posts/justin-welsh.md) (1,222 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: Concrete operational ritual. A Growth role running founder-led content should calendar-block the CEO this way, not "encourage posting."

## 4. Specific always outranks generic

> "Be that guy. Be the guy who solves a very specific problem that's painful for a very specific company or person... I am really specific on solopreneurship. Where you don't have a specific audience, you might have a harder time as your audience grows."

- **Source**: [transcript: How I Built an $8M Business with 0 Employees](../youtube-transcripts/justin-welsh--how-i-built-an-8m-business-with-0-employees.md)
- **Why it matters**: For 100Hires — "ATS for bootstrapped SMB" is specific; "HR tech" is generic. The specific angle is the one that compounds in the Interest Graph era.

## 5. Funnel reframed: Discovery → Trust → Relationship → Monetization

> "Get discovered, build trust, deepen relationships, and monetize. Getting discovered is organic social... Trust is long-form content, newsletter, a robust YouTube video... Relationship is events, communities... Monetization follows."

- **Source**: [transcript: How I Built an $8M Business with 0 Employees](../youtube-transcripts/justin-welsh--how-i-built-an-8m-business-with-0-employees.md)
- **Why it matters**: Replaces AIDA / TOFU-MOFU-BOFU with a creator funnel that maps cleanly onto what a lean B2B SaaS can actually execute on LinkedIn + email.

## 6. Pick an enemy, build a movement

> "You want to give people you want to help people throw rocks at their enemies... I throw rocks at the traditional career path. By throwing rocks at that enemy I am building a movement."

- **Source**: [transcript: How I Built an $8M Business with 0 Employees](../youtube-transcripts/justin-welsh--how-i-built-an-8m-business-with-0-employees.md)
- **Why it matters**: Gives founder content a backbone. For 100Hires, the enemy is obvious: bloated enterprise ATS with 6-week setup cycles and 10x the price.

## 7. The solo-plus-AI clarity moat

> "One person with a laptop is about to outcompete companies with 50 employees... Clarity. Companies can't move like you can. They have meetings about meetings. They need 6 approvals to change a headline. They spend Q1 'planning' what a solo operator shipped last Tuesday."

- **Source**: [Post 7, 2026-02-20](../linkedin-posts/justin-welsh.md) (1,727 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: Reinforces why a bootstrapped 11-person team competing against Greenhouse/Lever has a structural speed advantage — if it uses it.
