## 1. LinkedIn organic is the entire top of funnel — not a support channel

> "RB2B would not exist, be at ~$8m ARR in 24mo (run by 3 people), and be growing at 7%/mo if it were not for posting on LinkedIn. We literally launched the product because of how viral my B2B posts were going."

- **Source**: [Post 6, 2026-03-08](../linkedin-posts/adam-robinson.md) (402 reactions, 284 comments)
- **Why it matters**: The only "full-stack" LinkedIn case study in B2B SaaS where LinkedIn isn't a marketing channel — it's the origin of product-market fit. Demonstrates a ceiling few other B2B companies have even attempted.

## 2. If the hook fails, nothing else matters

> "Hook, Hook, Hook. The best post idea doesn't get written unless there is a great hook. If you don't get people to click 'see more' in the first line, nothing else matters."

- **Source**: [Post 6, 2026-03-08](../linkedin-posts/adam-robinson.md)
- **Why it matters**: LinkedIn shows ~2 lines before the "see more" fold. Every post should be optimized at that seam before any other edit. Concrete bar for content review.

## 3. Story beats data — even when you have data

> "Storytelling is everything. One of my competitors posts a monthly build-in-public that talks about what happened that month at the company. When I give a monthly update, I talk about my emotional journey being at the head of a $30m ARR company... with very little mention of what we actually did. Guess which one does better. Story > Everything."

- **Source**: [Post 6, 2026-03-08](../linkedin-posts/adam-robinson.md)
- **Why it matters**: Bootstrapped founders default to numbers (ARR, metrics, benchmarks). Adam's evidence is that the narrative wrapper outperforms the number itself — even with the number attached.

## 4. Write as if nobody has ever heard of you

> "Treat every post as though nobody has ever heard of you before. Why will they keep reading? I say things like 'I bootstrapped $0-$1m ARR 3x, and ______', or my personal favorite, Amos's line... 'when you're scaling a $30m ARR company with 3 people'... when he had less than $1m ARR!"

- **Source**: [Post 6, 2026-03-08](../linkedin-posts/adam-robinson.md)
- **Why it matters**: Interest Graph means new eyeballs every post. Credibility hooks (proof + specificity) in line 1-2 decide whether the rest gets read.

## 5. Edu-tainment: every post needs a lesson wrapped in a dopamine hit

> "If you're posting on LinkedIn, you're in the edu-tainment business. Equal parts 'edu' and 'tainment'. Every post needs a lesson, and you should wrap every lesson with something coy that gives the reader a dopamine hit, which gets them to engage."

- **Source**: [Post 6, 2026-03-08](../linkedin-posts/adam-robinson.md)
- **Why it matters**: Purely educational posts underperform; purely entertaining posts don't convert. The 50/50 mix is operable — gives a Growth hire a concrete test for every draft.

## 6. Pre-PMF, only four things matter

> "Until you have PMF, there are only 4 things you should be doing: talking to customers, improving the product, creating content, finding more customers to talk to. That's it."

- **Source**: [Post 5, 2026-03-14](../linkedin-posts/adam-robinson.md) (214 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: Content is on the very short list. Removes the false tradeoff of "we'll invest in content once we have PMF." Content is part of the PMF search.

## 7. Focus is making one choice so aggressive everything else becomes obvious

> "Focus means making a few choices so aggressive that everything else becomes obvious. One channel. Your creative gets better faster. One audience. Your messaging sounds like you're reading their mind. One offer. Your sales team stops freelancing."

- **Source**: [Post 7, 2026-02-24](../linkedin-posts/adam-robinson.md) (256 reactions)
- **Why it matters**: For a new Growth function at 100Hires — the decision isn't "which channels?" but "which one do we dominate?" Adam's answer: LinkedIn organic, founder-led.
